# The Engineering Manager Role

James Samuel frames the engineering manager as existing to **amplify the collective
impact** of a team by removing the obstacles to its effectiveness. Management layers
are not overhead to be flattened away: as an organisation grows, someone has to
coordinate communication, prioritisation, and alignment, and that coordination does
not happen for free. He organises the role into four pillars.

## The four pillars

| Pillar | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **People Leadership** | Hiring, onboarding, career development, and culture. Reading individual motivation (some engineers want autonomy, others want structure); coaching, conflict resolution, and delivering feedback. |
| **Technical Leadership** | Guiding architectural decisions and technical health *without necessarily being the strongest coder* — asking the right questions, balancing elegance against business value, and managing technical debt. |
| **Product Leadership** | Partnering with product management so the team solves the *right* problem; bridging customer value and technical feasibility; helping evaluate scope and validate assumptions before a major investment. |
| **Delivery Leadership** | The "operating system for software development" — the process, planning, and communication that turn ideas into shipped software; removing friction, managing uncertainty, and keeping information flowing between engineers, product, and stakeholders. |

## How the pillars balance

- **The balance shifts with context.** A platform migration leans on technical
  leadership; a period of rapid growth leans on people leadership. No fixed weighting
  is correct across situations.
- **The framework matters less than the outcome.** Samuel's caution is that the
  taxonomy is a lens, not a scorecard — what matters is whether the manager's
  processes actually help the team deliver quality software.
- **The core job is obstacle removal.** Underneath all four pillars, the manager's
  recurring task is to identify what is preventing the team from succeeding and clear it.
