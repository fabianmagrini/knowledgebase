# Principal Engineer Influence

At a senior level, influence outruns authority: people don't just weigh your ideas, they
**infer direction from your words**. Two practices follow from that — signal strategy
deliberately, and time feedback so forums validate rather than surprise.

> You are effectively a **living extension of the strategy**, whether you intend to be or not.

This is the strategy-and-timing companion to
[facilitating technical decisions](decision-facilitation.md), and it operationalises the
"no surprise objections" rule of the [architecture decision forum](architecture-decision-forum.md).

## Your words signal direction

Teams read casual remarks as directional signals — often overriding formal documentation.
So carry the strategy in the framing, and mark deviations explicitly as exceptions rather
than letting them read as a new default.

| Signals alignment | Quietly fragments direction |
|---|---|
| "Given our strategy of X, the default would be Y…" | "We could just…" |
| "The trade-off we're making here is…" | "It doesn't really matter here…" |
| "This is an exception because…" | "Let's not overthink it…" |

A pragmatic shortcut said casually becomes a precedent other teams copy. If you mean it as a
one-off trade-off, **say so**.

## Give feedback early and privately

High-stakes forums should validate thinking, not be where a major objection lands for the
first time. Surfacing a strong concern publicly and late undermines the owner and costs team
cohesion — for no benefit you couldn't get earlier.

1. **Engage early** — review designs proactively, before they harden.
2. **Frame as collaboration** — ask clarifying questions rather than declaring problems.
3. **Align privately first** — resolve disagreements before the public presentation.
4. **Support publicly** — reinforce strengths in the room; introduce no surprise objections.

> If you have a strong concern, the owner should hear it from you first — privately and early.

## Pre-forum checklist

Before speaking in a high-stakes setting:

- Is this aligned with the stated strategy?
- Am I clearly framing any deviation as an intentional exception?
- What behaviour will others copy from this?
- Have I already shared every major concern with the owner — would anything I say surprise
  them?
- Am I helping them succeed, or correcting them publicly?
